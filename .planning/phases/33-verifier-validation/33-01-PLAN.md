**scope-context.md** — A synthetic but realistic SCOPE.md that serves as upstream context for all test artifacts. This is the "scope" that design/spike/synthesis artifacts reference. Make it realistic enough that evidence citations in test artifacts can point back to it.

**strong-design.md** (expected: Go) — A well-crafted design document for G3 evaluation. Must have:
- Every design decision cites specific evidence from scope-context.md DAs by reference (e.g., "Per DA-1 research finding F3, CRDTs provide...")
- Consistent terminology throughout (same terms for same concepts)
- Explicit assumptions labeled as such with confidence levels (High/Medium/Low) that are honestly calibrated
- Complete reasoning chains: evidence -> analysis -> trade-offs considered -> decision -> rationale
- Alternative approaches discussed with reasons for rejection
- All sections follow a declared format consistently
- Target: all 4 dimensions should score 4-5

**adequate-design.md** (expected: Go-with-advisory) — A competent but imperfect design document for G3 evaluation. Must have:
- Most decisions cite evidence, but 1-2 decisions use vague citations ("research showed that..." instead of specific references)
- Generally consistent terminology with 1 minor inconsistency (using "sync" and "synchronization" interchangeably in a way that creates slight ambiguity)
- Assumptions mostly stated but 1 hidden assumption (e.g., assumes network latency under 200ms without stating it)
- Reasoning is adequate but 1 trade-off is mentioned without full analysis
- Target: most dimensions should score 3-4, producing go-with-advisory

**weak-design.md** (expected: Recycle) — A poor design document for G3 evaluation. Must have:
- Multiple unsupported claims (decisions stated as facts without evidence)
- At least 1 logical contradiction between sections (e.g., "we chose eventual consistency" in one section, "strong consistency is required" in another)
- Hidden assumptions throughout (latency, scale, user behavior assumed but never stated)
- Incomplete reasoning: jumps from problem to solution without analysis, no alternatives considered
- Evidence citations that are fabricated (reference findings that do not exist in scope-context.md)
- Target: at least 1-2 dimensions should score below 3, producing recycle

**borderline-spike.md** (expected: Go-with-advisory or Recycle) — A mixed-quality spike output for G5 evaluation. Must have:
- 2 tactical decisions resolved with solid evidence, 1 resolved with weak/missing evidence
- Implementation steps that are partially actionable (some specific, some vague)
- Sound reasoning on the well-evidenced decisions, hand-waving on the weak one
- Honest about 1 gap but ignores another
- Target: should land on the boundary between go-with-advisory and recycle (dimensions around 3)

**fabricated-synthesis.md** (expected: Recycle) — A research synthesis readiness assessment for G2-semantic evaluation. Must have:
- Sufficiency ratings (COVERED/PARTIAL/NOT COVERED) that are inflated relative to the actual evidence described
- Claims of "Strong corroboration" when only 1 source is cited
- "COVERED" ratings on questions where the evidence described is clearly weak or tangential
- Evidence citations that sound authoritative but are vague ("multiple industry sources confirm...")
- The evidence descriptions should make it obvious (to a careful reader) that the ratings are unjustified
- Target: evidence_support should score very low (1-2), overall recycle

Each fixture should be 80-200 lines. They are synthetic but must be realistic enough to exercise the verifier's judgment. The quality differences must be GENUINE (actually present in the text), not just labeled.
